Snapsassin
A mobile app to moderate and play Assassin games with face recognition assassinations.
No one has to sit out to moderate the game--Everyone can play!
Rather than shooting each other with squirt guns or throwing dirty socks around, this game is played with your phone camera. Snap a photo of your target and their identity will be verified using face recognition.
If that's the right person, you've successfully assassinated your target!
Second-place winner of Tufts PolyHack 2016

Appidemic
A real-time, location-based, massively multi-user outbreak simulator.
Currently only on Android, Appidemic is an app that tracks your health status.
As you go about your day, if you come into the vicinity of someone who is infected, you get infected!
Infection spread and status can be seen on the web client, which is a map of the world with all users displayed.
This would be an awesome zombie apocalypse simulation game or a perfect tool to study epidemics using real movement based on crowd-sourced information. Probably my best idea yet, although optimizing location tracking and keeping background services alive is kinda difficult (GPS would have to be on all the time; poor battery).

SueChef
Sue is your voice-activated cooking assistant.
Hands are covered in oil but you need to follow a recipe?
Don't touch your phone with those greasy fingers, just call out "Okay, sous-chef" and tell Sue what you need.
Based on Recipio, a web app that I worked on with the same purpose that won second place at the BridgeHaCS 2016 hackathon. Currently Android-only and in production.
